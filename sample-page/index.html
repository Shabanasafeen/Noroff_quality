Quality in topics

Quality in topics

On this page you will find information about how Noroff works with educational quality.

Quality work at course level must ensure that all courses offered are up-to-date and relevant, have adapted learning activities and that the students achieve the learning outcome that is determined for the individual course. There must be a good correspondence between learning outcomes, forms of learning and forms of assessment.

Course coordinator (s) are responsible for the quality work at course level.

Tools for quality work at course level

All courses must be evaluated each time they are completed. We distinguish between two types of evaluations: evaluations carried out during a course (mid-term evaluation) evaluations that are carried out at the end (final evaluation) In the educations at bachelor’s and master’s level, all students shall have the opportunity to evaluate all subjects either during or at the end. However, anonymous final evaluation must be carried out at least every third time a course is completed. After mid-term evaluations, the course coordinator will in a short time give the student group feedback in Canvas, which summarizes the mid-term evaluation and how this is followed up. A suitable evaluation method is selected based on the class / group’s size and needs. The toolbox for evaluations contains an overview of different evaluation methods and question formulations. See toolbox In the PhD programs, the courses in the training part of the individual PhD program shall be evaluated after each teaching period has ended. The scope and method of the evaluation must be adapted to the PhD program and the number of PhD candidates.

Supervised practice must be evaluated by both the students and the practice site. As a general rule, the evaluation must be carried out at the end of each internship period. Students will evaluate the internship period based on the established learning outcome for the internship period. The internship site will evaluate the experiences with the collaboration with Noroff, including routines for reception and professional follow-up of the students. In their evaluation, both the internship site and the student must propose improvement measures.

The students’ course evaluations, together with other relevant data, are summarized and analyzed in an annual course report. Minimum reporting requirements are set by the Department of Education in a reporting template The course coordinators’ own professional assessments must be included. Improvement work and proposals for measures to improve the quality of the course will be emphasized in the report. The student shop steward must confirm in writing that the report has been read and can also comment on the content. The course report, with main findings and a plan for further follow-up, shall be communicated back to both the student group that has evaluated and students in the next batch. This must be done in writing, in the form of publication in Canvas. The information should also be communicated orally.
